Climate change has had drastic impacts on the United States and the world in the past 30 years. We designed these visualizations to help users clearly see the worsening impact of climate change as observed through wildfires over the years.

U.S. Lightning Fire Size Over Time (1992 - 2015)

Here, users can see the overall trend over time of the average size of fires in acres. Users can observe from the line of best fit that the trend is increasing over time, indicating that fires are clearly getting bigger as the years progress.

Average Time to Extinguish Lightning Fires Each Year

This bar chart shows the typical increase in time taken to extinguish fires over the years. The upward trend indicates a decrease in the controllability of wildfires due to changing climate conditions (hotter temperatures, drier environments, etc.).

Wildfires by State in 1993 & 2011

US Wildfire Frequency by State (1993)

US Wildfire Frequency by State (2011)

Users can observe the data by state from these two different years. Clicking on states allows users to see specific values for those states, and the map can be moved around and viewed from different angles and locations. The wildfire frequency between these two years can clearly be seen as significantly different.
